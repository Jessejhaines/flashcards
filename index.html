Building Science Flashcards

Wet Zone
The portion of the wall from the drainage plane to the exterior face, designed to resist rain, snow, and temperature changes.
Dry Zone
The portion of the wall from the drainage plane to the interior face, not intended for prolonged water exposure.
Drainage Plane
A barrier within the wall assembly that prevents water and air penetration, separating the wet zone from the dry zone.
Advective Moisture Flow
Water movement caused by air carrying dissolved water through imperfections in the wall assembly.
Convective Moisture Flow
Water movement driven by temperature-induced buoyancy differences between warm and cold air.
Diffusive Moisture Flow
The transfer of moisture through materials, influenced by factors like permeability, pressure, and humidity differentials.
Wind-Driven Rain
Water penetration caused by the momentum of wind and gravity.
Gravity Flow
The natural downward movement of water on surfaces, managed by features like shingles and flashing.
Surface Tension
Cohesion among water molecules causing water to cling to surfaces, addressed by features like drip edges.
Capillary Action
Moisture absorption through small voids in materials such as concrete, wood, or gypsum.
Conduction
Heat transfer through direct contact between materials or particles.
Radiation
Heat transfer through electromagnetic waves between materials not in contact.
Convection
Heat transfer via circulatory motion of warm and cool air or liquids.
State Changes
Processes like condensation, evaporation, melting, and freezing that impact building science.
Mass Walls
Thick, solid walls designed to deflect, absorb, and later evaporate moisture.
Barrier Walls
Walls that rely on weather-tight cladding to shed water without cavities for drainage.
Face-Seal EIFS
A system where the lamina acts as the primary barrier to water but lacks cavities for drainage.
Cavity Walls
Walls with a cavity to allow for drainage and air circulation, managing moisture through evaporation and convection.
Infiltration & Exfiltration
Uncontrolled air movement into (infiltration) and out of (exfiltration) a building due to ineffective barriers.
Storage Capacity
The ability of materials in the wet zone to absorb and evaporate moisture without degrading.
Thermal Barriers
Materials designed to reduce heat transfer, maximizing R-value for insulation.
